What is Create Now?

Create Now 2013 is a premier leadership event in the Norman and Greater OKC metro area hosted by JourneyChurch and Norman NEXT. This is our third-annual leadership event.
Norman NEXT is an organization for young professionals, 21-40 years of age, working or living in Norman, OK with a mission to develop the next generation of leaders for Norman by creating a connected, active and engaged community of young professionals.
JourneyChurch is a house where God is honored, where prayers are answered, hearts are mended and lives are changed. A house committed to transforming our cities one life at a time. A house that uses creativity to point people to the Creator. A house that believes the God of generations past is the answer for the next generation. The people of JourneyChurch gather to build healthy relationships and have a blast together. The values of JourneyChurch include influence and creativity, targeting the youngest generation alive.
The purpose of Create Now 2013 is to teach and impact the next generation of leaders while providing an opportunity for existing leaders to grow and be challenged by successful, creative, influential people in the marketplace. Create Now 2013 will encourage leadership, positive conversations, and creativity in our culture. Those who attend have the opportunity to learn, and walk away inspired and empowered to live into their greatest potential.

Roy Spence



The Keynote speaker this year is Roy Spence. Roy is Co-Founder and Chairman of GSD&M, a national and leading marketing communications and advertising company and co-authored the Wall Street Journal bestselling book, It’s Not What You Sell, It’s What You Stand For: Why Every Extraordinary Business is Driven by Purpose.

Under Roy’s leadership, his agency has helped grow some of the world’s most successful brands like “Don’t Mess with Texas”, Southwest Airlines, Wal-Mart, DreamWorks, the PGA TOUR, BMW, the U.S. Air Force, LLBean and the Clinton Global Initiative. Roy has been a trusted advisor to legendary leaders including Sam Walton and Southwest Airlines founder, Herb Kelleher. His counsel has also been sought by U.S. presidents and leaders in the State Department.
